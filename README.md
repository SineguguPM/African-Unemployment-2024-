This project presents a data-driven analysis of unemployment patterns across Africa, using 2024 country-level data to identify key drivers, demographic disparities, and structural labour market challenges. The insights are visualized in an interactive Power BI dashboard.
Objectives:
The goals of this project are to:
•Analyse unemployment trends across Africa
•Identify regional and demographic patterns in unemployment
•Determine key drivers of unemployment
•Identify gaps between workforce skills and employer demands
•Use tools to extract patterns and generate insights

Data Description:
•Source: The dataset used in this analysis is based on 2024 country-level unemployment data sourced from The Global Economy (https://www.theglobaleconomy.com/rankings/unemployment_rate/Africa/), which provides country-level unemployment rates across African nations for the year 2024.

Analysis and Metrics
•Regional Trends: The analysis reveals significant regional disparities in unemployment rates. Southern Africa has the highest average unemployment rate at 17.9%, while West Africa records the lowest at 5.2%. This suggests that structural economic differences and varying levels of industrial development influence employment opportunities across regions.
•Age Group Analysis :Youth aged 15–24 experience the highest unemployment rate at 20%, making them the most vulnerable group. This indicates that first-time job seekers face structural barriers to entry into the labour market.
•Education Level Analysis: An unexpected finding is that individuals with tertiary education have higher unemployment rates (15.3%) compared to those with lower education levels. This highlights a critical mismatch between academic training and industry requirements.
•Gender Analysis: Women experience higher unemployment (11.5%) compared to men (9.9%). This gap reflects broader socio-economic challenges, including limited access to opportunities and structural barriers.
•Digital Access: Individuals without internet access have higher unemployment rates compared to those with access. This demonstrates that digital connectivity plays a key role in improving employability.
•Skills Gap Analysis: The analysis identifies a significant shortage of vocational and technical skills, with skills-related factors contributing heavily to unemployment. This suggests that many job seekers lack practical, job-ready skills required by employers, reinforcing the need for skills development programs. Skills gap insights were derived through a combination of data patterns and AI-assisted interpretation of labour market trends.

These findings demonstrate that unemployment in Africa is not solely a job availability issue, but a combination of structural, informational, and skills-related challenges.

Key Insights:
•Youth Employment Crisis: Young people aged 15–24 experience the highest unemployment rates (~20%), highlighting barriers to labour market entry.
•Education–Employment Mismatch: Individuals with tertiary education show higher unemployment rates than those with lower education levels, indicating a disconnect between academic training and labour market demand.
•Information and Access Gap: Factors such as poor career awareness and limited internet access contribute significantly to unemployment, suggesting that access to information and opportunity plays a critical role.

Recommendations:
Based on the findings, the following recommendations are proposed:
1. Implement Youth Employment Programs:
  •Industries must be encouraged to implement apprenticeships and internships, and provide entry-level job opportunities
2. Expand vocational and digital skills training aligned with high-demand sectors identified in the analysis (e.g., technical and digital roles):
  •Increase TVET institutions and encourage enrolment
  •School syllabi should focus on practical, industry-relevant skills
3. Implement Education Reform
  •Align academic curricula with labour market needs
  •Introduce industry partnerships
4. Expand Digital Inclusion
  •Governments need to expand internet access and promote digital literacy
5. Implement Gender-Inclusive Policies
  •Laws must be created in support of women in the workforce
  •Improve access to childcare and flexible work
6. Encourage Economic Diversification
  •Reduce reliance on only some industries
  •Promote entrepreneurship and innovation
